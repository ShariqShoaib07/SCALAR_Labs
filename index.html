About

I graduated with a PhD in robotics with focus on control, motion planning, and learning of robotic systems. My PhD research includes designing safety-compliant algorithms for robotic systems with both parametric and environment uncertainty.

I also graduated with an MBA as a dual degree candidate at Scheller College of Business, Georgia Institute of Technology, specializing in product management, business analytics, and strategy and innovation. At Georgia Tech, I am a recipient of several awards including Fulbright Scholarship, GALT Graduate, and A.J. Lynne Fellow.

My graduate research experience includes working on complex robots in challenging environments, including whole-body control of a 19-DoF wheel-inverted-pendulum humanoid robot, real-time safety and control of a 7-DoF robotic manipulator, optimal distributed and consensus control of multi-agent systems, and adaptive cruise control with safety and regulation constraints. I verified these algorithms on both physical hardware and simulation, with publications in top robotics and control venues.

I have worked with Matlab, Simulink, Robot Operating System (ROS), Gazebo, Drake, Dynamic Animation and Robotics Toolkit (DART), Eigen, OpenCV, PyTorch, and scikit-learn. My work has been published in ICRA, IROS, CASE, ISER, IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters, IEEE Control Systems Letters, and IEEE Transactions on Robotics.

In a previous role, I worked as an embedded software engineer at Mentor Graphics on Sourcery CodeBench, including compilers, runtime libraries, source and assembly-level debugger tools, and integrated development environments.

  • Current Focus: Control, safety, and learning for robotic systems in human-centered environments.
  • Research Areas: Motion planning, model predictive control, multi-robot systems, and LLM-enabled robot planning.
  • Technical Stack: ROS, Gazebo, Drake, DART, Matlab, Simulink, Python, C++, PyTorch, OpenCV, Eigen.
